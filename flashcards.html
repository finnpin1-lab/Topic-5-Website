[LOG_01: PROTECTION]

There are many methods of protecting a network. Examples: Access Control, Physical Security, Firewalls


ACCESS CONTROL: 

            -Limits who can access

            -Users may only view certain files

            -Users can be given 'Read-Only Access'

            -Users can be given right to delete files

PHYSICAL SECURITY:

            -Access doors should be kept locked with keypads or other methods

            -Swipe cards can be used for identification

            -RFID chips can be fitted to sound an alarm if removed

FIREWALL:
            -Protects a network connected to a WAN

            -Can be provided by hardware or software

            -Can be configured to prevent communications in and out
💡 Data Tip: Individual computers are likely to have software-based firewalls, whereas a large business is more likely to have a hardware-based one.

[LOG_02: ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECTS]

The advancing of technology has had many adverse effects on the environment caused by Manufacturing and Disposal


Manufacturing:

                -Large quantities of rare materials used

                -Non-renewable materials used. e.g. Palladium and Copper

                -Toxic Materials used. e.g. Arsenic.

                -mining for raw materials scars landscape

                -Much of the energy used comes from fossil fuels#

Disposal:
                -50 million tons of E-Waste produced annually

                -only abound 20% of e-waste is recycled

                -E-waste can be dumped in landfill sites where toxic materials leak into environment

Energy Use:
                
                -Approximately 70% of energy is used in Manufacturing

                -Approximately 30% is used in device use
💡 Data Tip: Responsible recycling can address many of the problems with e-waste. e.g: reduces potential for chemical leakage, enables the recovery of valuable materials and reduces the number harmful toxins released into the air.

[LOG_03: AI]

AI has been a hot topic in recent years with it's development. There are many different types of AI.


Types:
                -Machine Learning: Learns by looking for rules and patterns in data. They get progressively better over time.
                    
                -Narrow AI: Designed with a specific task in mind. Narrow AI is programmed to do one thing and cannot transfer that knowledge to different tasks.

Problems:
                -Algorithmic Bias: Can happen due to biased datasets, design flaws in the code - causing exaggeration

                -Responsibility: It can be hard to know who to blame if something goes wrong. Anybody - from the user, to the original creator, to the one who supplied the datasets can be blamed for problems.
💡 Data Tip: There are many types of narrow AI - for example, email spam filters; social media monitoring tools; fingerprint recognition; content reccomendation and autonomous weapon systems.
